Data Origins

Our dataset comes from the U.S. Department of Education, specifically the EDFacts initiative. They collect graduation rate data from school districts throughout the country, and we are using data from the 2019-2020 school year. For each school district that provided data, EDFacts recorded their state, district name, and a graduation rate for each of 12 student cohorts as well as the size of those cohorts.

Modifications to the Original Data

  • Initially, the CSV data file had 5 extraneous columns. To begin with, we deleted the year and date fields, since they remained constant throughout the entire dataset with the year 2019-2020 and the date 19MAY21. Then, we also deleted 3 IDs associated with states and districts, as we already had the state names and the district names.
  • Since the “rate” field of the data was often given as a range (ex. “50-60” or “GE50”) for ease of look-up, we added a column with a single converted rate stored that could be stored as a float. For ranges such as “50-60” we took the average, storing 55.0 as the graduation rate. Similarly for rates “GE50” we stored 75.0.
  • Some graduation rates were privacy suppressed because they were not large enough cohorts for anonymity. In these cases, we set our converted rate to 0.0. Note that we did not include any of the privacy-suppressed values in our average calculations.

Data Limitations

The only two states/territories that are not recorded in their entirety are Texas and Illinois, since Texas never agreed to provide their data while Illinois provided data with too many errors. Georgia also had issues with their data submission, but only the Foster Care Students cohort was impacted, and thus not included in the data.
